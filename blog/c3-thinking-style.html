Cultural Blog 3: Learning Styles

August 16, 2015

According to the quiz, my thinking style is firmly within the Abstract Sequential category. While I really don't think that these thinking styles are as clear-cut as the internet/test makes them out to be, this would imply that I like to think about abstract and subtle things in a linear, logical manner. So far I have found that applying a logical or linear framework to solving complex problems (one of the hallmarks of AS thinkers) is very well suited for computer science. This is one explanation for why I have enjoy programming so much--I like coming up with solutions to logic puzzels. Like typical AS thinkers, I enjoy stimulating work and I can work alone, both of which are beneficial in phase 0.

One thing that has been difficult for me is sticking with the driver/navigator roles during pairing. This makes sense within the context of my thinking style as I tend to dislike too many little rules/regulatins (ie: don't touch the keyboard if you are navigating). Also, working with people who view things differently AND can't give a logical explanation as to why can be frustrating.

That is all for now. See you next time.
